Day-to-Day Physiotherapy Exercises

1. Deep Breathing Exercise

Procedure: Sit or stand comfortably. Inhale deeply through your nose, filling your lungs with air. Hold your breath for a few seconds, then exhale slowly through your mouth, emptying your lungs completely.

Uses: Deep breathing exercises help improve lung capacity, increase oxygen intake, reduce stress, and promote relaxation.

2. Neck Stretching

Procedure: Sit or stand tall. Slowly tilt your head to one side, bringing your ear towards your shoulder. Hold for 15-30 seconds, then repeat on the other side.

Uses: Neck stretching exercises help relieve tension, improve flexibility, and reduce stiffness in the neck muscles.

3. Shoulder Rolls

Procedure: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart. Roll your shoulders forward in a circular motion, making big circles. Repeat for 10-15 times, then reverse the direction and roll your shoulders backward.

Uses: Shoulder rolls help improve shoulder mobility, relieve tension in the shoulder muscles, and prevent stiffness.

4. Leg Raises

Procedure: Lie flat on your back with your legs straight. Lift one leg off the ground, keeping it straight, until it reaches a 45-degree angle with the floor. Hold for a few seconds, then slowly lower it back down. Repeat with the other leg.

Uses: Leg raises strengthen the abdominal muscles, hip flexors, and quadriceps, improve core stability, and enhance balance.

5. Ankle Circles

Procedure: Sit on a chair with your feet flat on the floor. Lift one foot off the ground and rotate your ankle in a circular motion, first clockwise and then counterclockwise. Repeat with the other foot.

Uses: Ankle circles help improve ankle flexibility, reduce stiffness, and prevent ankle injuries.
